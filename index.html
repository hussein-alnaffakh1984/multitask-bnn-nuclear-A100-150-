Case Study

Tin Isotopic Chain (Z = 50)

The tin chain is the centerpiece of this study. The famous B(E2) plateau between A = 110 and 124 is reproduced by the BNN within one standard deviation. Beyond A = 132 the uncertainty band broadens, reflecting the absence of training data in the neutron-rich region.

Quality Assurance

Validation on 10 Reference Nuclei

BNN predictions tested against ten well-measured reference nuclei from across the studied region. Deviations are reported in standard deviations (σ). Coverage at 2σ reaches 100% for mass excess, S(2n), E(2⁺), E(4⁺), and B(E2). The famous ¹³²Sn doubly-magic outlier is correctly flagged with widened uncertainty.

Scientific Background

About this Work

Methodology

We developed a multi-task Bayesian neural network ensemble that jointly predicts fourteen nuclear observables. The architecture combines a deterministic shared trunk (64→32 neurons, ReLU, dropout 0.1) with fourteen task-specific Bayesian heads (16-neuron Bayesian dense + Bayesian linear projection). Variational inference uses mean-field Gaussian guides via Pyro 1.9. Five BNNs (seeds 42, 123, 456, 789, 1024) are trained for 2000 epochs each on a Tesla T4 GPU and combined into a deep ensemble.

Data Sources

  • AME2020 [Wang et al. 2021] — mass excess and S(2n) for 651 nuclei
  • Pritychenko 2016 [Pritychenko et al.] — B(E2; 0⁺→2⁺) and E(2⁺) for 134 even-even nuclei
  • NUDAT3 [NNDC, BNL] — first 4⁺ excitation energies for 141 even-even nuclei
  • R(4/2) computed directly from E(4⁺)/E(2⁺)
